🔥 FireBot F95 - Complete Step-by-Step Build & Working Procedure

Step 1: Understanding the System 
Before building, the team reviews the complete working of FireBot F95.
* FireBot continuously scans its surroundings using flame and infrared sensors.
* When fire is detected, it identifies the direction and center (centroid).
* The robot moves toward the fire while avoiding obstacles.
* A water jet sprinkler is activated to extinguish the fire.
* Alerts are generated and monitoring data is displayed.
This ensures all team members share a common understanding.

Step 2: Finalizing Components & Layout 
The team finalizes all components and their placement:
* Chassis at the base
* Motors and wheels on sides
* Battery and controller at center
* Flame sensors placed around the robot for 360° detection
* Ultrasonic sensor at the front
* Water tank and pump on the upper platform
* Servo-mounted nozzle at the front/top
A rough hand-drawn layout is sufficient.

Step 3: Assembling the Base Structure 
* Fix motors firmly to the chassis.
* Attach wheels and ensure free rotation.
* Mount battery and motor driver securely.
* Verify that the robot moves straight and turns correctly.
At this stage, the robot should move smoothly when powered.

Step 4: Building the Upper Body 
* Construct a lightweight box using cardboard or thermocol.
* Provide openings for sensors, nozzle, and wiring.
* Line inner surfaces with aluminium foil for heat protection.
* Secure the water tank and pump to avoid vibration.
The body should be stable, lightweight, and accessible.

Step 5: Sensor Installation & Wiring 
* Install flame sensors around the robot for full coverage.
* Fix ultrasonic sensor at front for obstacle detection.
* Mount infrared / temperature sensor for heat intensity sensing.
* Add buzzer and indicator LEDs.
* Complete wiring carefully and label connections.
Ensure no loose wires or short circuits.

Step 6: Controller Programming & Logic Development 
* Upload base motor control code to Arduino.
* Add logic for:
o Fire detection
o Direction determination
o Obstacle avoidance
* Implement centroid-based targeting so the nozzle aligns with fire center.
* Program pump activation and servo control.
* Add manual override using Bluetooth (optional).
Test each logic section independently.

Step 7: Fire Extinguishing Mechanism Setup 
* Connect pump through relay module.
* Align nozzle using servo motor.
* Adjust water flow rate to avoid splashing.
* Ensure water reaches flame safely and accurately.
Test with small controlled flames only.

Step 8: Alerts & Monitoring Integration 
* Configure buzzer to activate on fire detection.
* Display temperature level and fire intensity.
* Monitor water percentage and alert when low.
* Enable live camera feed and night vision (if included).
* Configure mobile alerts if supported.
This improves awareness and safety.

Step 9: Testing, Debugging & Optimization 
* Test movement without fire.
* Test fire detection sensitivity.
* Test obstacle avoidance accuracy.
* Test extinguishing performance.
* Adjust delays, speed, and sensor thresholds.
Repeat tests until performance is reliable.

Step 10: Final Demonstration Preparation 
* Clean wiring and secure all components.
* Prepare PPT and explanation flow.
* Practice live demo sequence.
* Assign speaking points for presentation.
* Ensure safety measures during demo.
The robot should be stable, predictable, and safe.
